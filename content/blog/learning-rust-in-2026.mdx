# Learning Rust in 2026

_March 1, 2026_

I picked up Rust about a year ago. Here are my unfiltered thoughts.

## The good

- **The compiler is your pair programmer.** If it compiles, it's usually correct. The error messages are genuinely helpful.
- **No null.** `Option<T>` replaces null and you can't forget to handle it.
- **Traits > inheritance.** Cleaner, more explicit, no diamond problem.

## The hard

- **Borrow checker.** After a year I can work with it, but ergonomic patterns (trees, graphs, self-referential structs) still require hacks like arena allocation or `unsafe`.
- **Compile times.** Even with incremental compilation, a medium-sized project takes 20-30 seconds for a clean build.
- **Ecosystem fragmentation.** `tokio` vs `async-std`, `axum` vs `actix`, `diesel` vs `sqlx` — too many equally-good options.

## Verdict

Worth it. I write Rust for CLI tools, systems programming, and anything where I'd reach for C++.
